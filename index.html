Types of Art Styles

Within the industry of Art. Styles and views on art is very so often changed or newly discovered. We will talk about a couple Art styles which are popularised or very well known through the buisness.

Pop Art

Pop Art is defined as art based on modern popular culture and the mass media. In my understanding Pop Art is basically art that portrays today’s world. Pop art has various versions and examples of itself. But overall art is what you create it to be. Some subjects that incorporated pop art were commonplace objects. Examples would be comic strips as it uses various bright colors with an aesthetic of a comic book. Most pop art comic books use warhol dots. Abstract Art

Abstract art is an explosion of creativity put into a canvas, it is to represent external reality with its effective shapes, forms, colors, and textures. Abstract art never takes on an accurate depiction of an object, form, or reality but uses effects and imagination to achieve its goal. In the 1940’s abstract art movement began and after world war 2 is when the first real abstract art was painted and evolved.

Modern Art

Modern Art is realistic art which is a rejection of history and innovation with form (shapes, colors, and the lines that make up the work). Modern art as well includes work produced within the 1860’s to the 1970’s. Modern art was influenced by other types of movements such as impressionism, cubism, expressionism, and surrealism. Many famous paintings were modern art such as the ‘The Starry Night’ by Vincent van Gogh and ‘The Scream’ by Edward Munch.

Graffiti

Graffiti is not considered an art style but more of an art form. Though it may be a form, I cannot deny it’s rise in popularity and use in the culture. Many artists have been popularised due to their work on walls with graffiti. Artists such as Banksy, David Choe, Blek Le Rat, and Eduardo Kobra. Graffiti has been a big part of Art and influences many works and pieces.
